## Diagnosis

142 real pages are stuck purely on **crawl priority/budget** — a young, low-authority
domain handed ~190 new URLs at once by the consolidation, with 213 thin taxonomy pages
competing for crawl budget. Not a technical-indexability fault.

The one genuine code gap found: **sitemap emitted no `<lastmod>`** (0 entries), so Google
had no freshness signal to prioritize the stuck URLs.

### Ruled out
- Redirect explosion (the 62 `/articles/` 301s → "Page with redirect" = 3, not the 355).
- Orionfold JSON-LD / branding (metadata change doesn't create discovered-not-indexed).
- robots.txt / canonical / sitemap-domain bugs (all correct).

## Fixes applied

1. `public/robots.txt`: `Disallow: /field-notes/tags/` + `/field-notes/stages/`.
2. `rel="nofollow"` on tag/stage links: `src/pages/field-notes/[slug]/index.astro` (×2),
   `src/components/field-notes/StageFilter.astro`, `src/components/field-notes/ProjectStats.astro`.
3. `astro.config.mjs`: per-article `<lastmod>` from frontmatter dates (62 entries, varied
   2026-04-09 → 2026-05-29) injected in the sitemap `serialize()`.
